Grammar Rules

The standardized rules used by this dictionary.

The phrases from the original image are preserved as classic expressions. Some are shortened slang, so they do not always show every standard grammar particle.

1. Basic Word Order

Zabian normally uses Verb–Object–Subject.

Vek zub zab.
see you I
“I see you.”

2. Pronouns

Pronouns do not change form for subject or object.

  • zab — I / me
  • zub — you
  • zif — he / him
  • zaf — she / her
  • zop — it
  • vab — we / us
  • vib — they / them
  • zun — you all

3. Tense Particles

Tense appears at the beginning. It may be omitted when time is obvious.

ba = past
bo = present/current
bi = future
Ba vek zub zab. = I saw you.
Bi tev zub. = You will come.

4. Negation

Place bleb immediately before the verb. With tense, tense comes first.

Bleb zup zab. = I do not know.
Bi bleb tev zub. = You will not come.

5. Questions

Add kep at the end. The normal word order remains unchanged.

Zap mip zub kep?
want water you question
“Do you want water?”

6. Question Words

Question words occupy the place where the answer would normally appear.

Vek zha zub kep? = What do you see?
Rov zhi zub kep? = Where are you going?

7. The Copula Is Usually Omitted

“Am,” “is,” and “are” are normally left out before adjectives and nouns.

Fiz zab. = I am happy.
Vip vop zub. = You are a friend.

es may be used for emphasis or formal identity statements.

8. Adjectives Follow Nouns

The noun comes first, followed by its description.

lom gor = big home
vip zil = beautiful person
ska dum = dark sky

9. Possession

Use na between the possessed thing and its owner.

lom na zab = my home
nam na zub = your name
buk na zif = his book

10. Plurals

Place ra before a noun. Numbers make ra optional.

ra vip = people
tri vip = three people
ra tar = stars

11. Commands

Use ha before a command. The subject is normally omitted.

Ha dor gat. = Open the door.
Ha rov lom. = Go home.

12. Adverbs

Manner words usually follow the verb. Time and location phrases usually come before the final subject.

Rov fas lom zab.
go quickly home I
“I go home quickly.”

13. Joining Ideas

ka means “and,” vo means “or,” and nu means “but.”

Zap dop ka mip zab.
“I want food and water.”

14. Conditions and Reasons

ke introduces “if.” ta introduces “because.”

Ke tev zub, fiz zab.
“If you come, I am happy.”

15. Comparison

Use ma for “more,” mu for “most,” and tan for “than.”

zil ma zaf tan zab
“She is more beautiful than me.”

16. Formal vs. Casual Speech

Casual speech drops obvious pronouns and particles. Classic expressions such as zap (“I want”) and zab zup (“I don’t know”) are accepted idioms.

17. Pronunciation

  • a as in “father”
  • e as in “bed”
  • i as in “machine”
  • o as in “go”
  • u as in “rule”
  • z, b, p, v, k, t, m, n, r, l use their normal English sounds

18. Stress

Stress the first syllable unless a word contains a hyphen. In a hyphenated compound, stress the first syllable of each part.

ZÁ-bian · ZÍB-zi-di · KÉP-ta
